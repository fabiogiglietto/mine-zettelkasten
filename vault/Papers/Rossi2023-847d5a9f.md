# Cracking Open the European Newsfeed

> Rossi, L., Giglietto, F., & Marino, G. (2023). Cracking Open the European Newsfeed. *Journal of Quantitative Description: Digital Media*. https://doi.org/10.51685/jqd.2023.020
>
> [View paper](https://doi.org/10.51685/jqd.2023.020)

## Summary

This paper extends Guess et al.'s (2021) US-focused study of news quality on Facebook to a European setting, analysing the prevalence, exposure, and sharing of trustworthy and untrustworthy URLs in France, Germany, and Italy from 2019 to 2022. Combining Meta's URL Shares Dataset with NewsGuard domain trustworthiness ratings, the authors show that while the total volume of shared URLs has declined, the *proportion* of untrustworthy URLs has risen sharply in Germany and Italy — particularly around election years — even as the proportion of *Views* from untrustworthy sources has remained relatively stable. The study confirms the cross-Atlantic generalisability of one robust pattern: older users disproportionately drive both exposure to and sharing of low-quality content. It also serves as a methodological reflection on the limits of platform-provided datasets in advance of Digital Services Act transparency obligations.

## Key Contributions

- First systematic comparative description of trustworthy vs. untrustworthy news circulation on Facebook across France, Germany, and Italy using Meta's privacy-protected URL Shares Dataset.
- Extends Guess et al. (2021) temporally (through 2022) and geographically to a European multi-country context.
- Methodological critique of the URL Shares Dataset's 100-share threshold and of using NewsGuard ratings longitudinally, including stability checks.
- Documents European age-based patterns of misinformation engagement, extending US findings on older users' outsized role.
- Provides empirical and methodological groundwork for evaluating DSA-era platform research access.

## Methods

- Replication and extension of Guess et al. (2021), adapting publicly available code (Aslett, 2022) to European contexts.
- Analysis of Meta's URL Shares Dataset v10.1 (Jan 2017–Oct 2022), filtered by `top_country` for DE, FR, IT, and US.
- Domains classified as Trustworthy (NewsGuard score ≥60) or Untrustworthy (<60) using the October 2022 snapshot of 8,506 rated domains, applied retrospectively.
- Coverage compared against the Lasser-Rupp list, Media Bias/Fact Check, and Grinberg et al. (2019) to assess representativeness; historical NewsGuard data used to verify rating stability (only ~1–3% of domains changed category).
- Confidence intervals computed to account for differential privacy noise (σ=14 for shares, σ=2,228 for views).
- Views and Shares disaggregated across six age cohorts (18–24 through 65+).

## Findings

- France consistently has the highest URL volume on Facebook, then Italy; Germany roughly half or less, consistent with lower Facebook news adoption there.
- In 2019, untrustworthy URL shares were similar (~13–14%) across the three countries; by 2022 they diverged sharply: France 11%, Germany ~21%, Italy 22%.
- Peaks in untrustworthy URL shares coincide with national election years in Germany (2021) and Italy (2022).
- Absolute Views and Shares for untrustworthy URLs peaked in 2020 and then declined alongside overall URL volume.
- The *percentage* of Views from untrustworthy sources remained stable or slightly declined despite rising untrustworthy URL proportions, suggesting platform-level dampening of exposure.
- Shares to untrustworthy domains rose substantially (Germany 22% in 2021; Italy 26% in 2022).
- Sharing of untrustworthy content skews older than exposure: dominated by 65+ in France (28%), 55–64 in Germany (36%), and 45–54 in Italy (33%).
- US data show a stronger correlation between NewsGuard untrustworthy-domain share and untrustworthy URLs in the dataset than European data, undermining direct US–EU comparison.
