# Cracking Open the European Newsfeed

> Rossi, L., Giglietto, F., & Marino, G. (2023). Cracking Open the European Newsfeed. *Journal of Quantitative Description: Digital Media*. https://doi.org/10.51685/jqd.2023.020
>
> [View paper](https://doi.org/10.51685/jqd.2023.020)

## Summary

This paper extends Guess et al.'s (2021) US-focused analysis of news quality on Facebook to a European context, examining the prevalence, exposure, and sharing of trustworthy and untrustworthy URLs in France, Germany, and Italy from 2019 to 2022. Drawing on Meta's privacy-protected URL Shares Dataset combined with NewsGuard domain ratings, the authors argue that while the total volume of URLs has steadily declined and the share of untrustworthy URLs has risen in Germany and Italy (notably during election years), actual exposure (Views) to untrustworthy content has remained comparatively stable. They further demonstrate that older users dominate both exposure and sharing of low-quality content, mirroring US patterns. The study doubles as a methodological critique of the data infrastructures available for European platform research.

## Key Contributions

- First systematic cross-national quantitative description of trustworthy vs. untrustworthy news circulation on Facebook across France, Germany, and Italy.
- Temporal and geographic extension of Guess et al. (2021), enabling within-Europe comparison through 2022.
- Methodological critique of the URL Shares Dataset's 100-share threshold and of using NewsGuard ratings longitudinally, including domain-level stability checks.
- Empirical confirmation that older users disproportionately drive engagement with untrustworthy content in European contexts.
- Implications for transparency and data-access provisions under the EU Digital Services Act.

## Methods

The authors replicate and adapt Guess et al. (2021), using Aslett's (2022) public code on Meta's URL Shares Dataset v10.1 (2017–2022), filtered by `top_country` for DE, FR, IT, and US. Domains are classified as Trustworthy (NewsGuard score ≥60) or Untrustworthy (<60) using an October 2022 snapshot of 8,506 rated domains, applied retrospectively. NewsGuard coverage is benchmarked against the Lasser-Rupp list, Media Bias/Fact Check, and Grinberg et al. (2019). Differential privacy noise is accounted for via confidence intervals (σ=14 for shares, σ=2,228 for views). Views and Shares are disaggregated across six age cohorts.

## Findings

- France has the highest URL volume, followed by Italy; Germany's volume is roughly half, consistent with lower Facebook news adoption there.
- Untrustworthy URL shares were comparable (~13–14%) across countries in 2019 but diverged by 2022: France 11%, Germany ~21%, Italy 22%, with peaks aligned to electoral years.
- Absolute Views and Shares for untrustworthy URLs peaked in 2020 then declined alongside the overall URL drop.
- The percentage of Views from untrustworthy sources remained stable or declined despite the rise in untrustworthy URLs, suggesting platform-level dampening of exposure.
- Sharing of untrustworthy content skews older than exposure: 65+ dominates in France (28%), 55–64 in Germany (36%), 45–54 in Italy (33%); users under 34 play a marginal role.
- US data show a stronger correlation between untrustworthy NewsGuard coverage and untrustworthy URLs than EU data, justifying EU-only comparative analysis.
- Only 1–3% of NewsGuard-rated domains changed category over 2019–2022, supporting retrospective classification.
