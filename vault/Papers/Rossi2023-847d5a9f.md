# Cracking Open the European Newsfeed

> Rossi, L., Giglietto, F., & Marino, G. (2023). Cracking Open the European Newsfeed. *Journal of Quantitative Description: Digital Media*. https://doi.org/10.51685/jqd.2023.020
>
> [View paper](https://doi.org/10.51685/jqd.2023.020)

## Summary

This paper extends Guess et al.'s (2021) US-focused analysis of news quality on Facebook to three major European countries — Germany, France, and Italy — over the 2019–2022 period. Combining Meta's URL Shares Dataset with NewsGuard domain trustworthiness ratings, the authors document a steady decline in overall URL sharing alongside a rising share of untrustworthy URLs in Germany and Italy (spiking in electoral years), even as the proportion of *Views* from untrustworthy sources remained comparatively stable. Older cohorts drive both exposure and sharing of untrustworthy content across all three countries. The paper doubles as a methodological reflection on the promises and limits of Meta's privacy-protected research data and NewsGuard's coverage.

## Key Contributions

- First systematic cross-national quantitative description of trustworthy vs. untrustworthy news circulation on Facebook in Germany, France, and Italy using Meta's URL Shares Dataset.
- Temporal and geographic extension of Guess et al. (2021), enabling within-EU comparison rather than US-centric inference.
- Methodological critique of the URL Shares Dataset (particularly the 100-share inclusion threshold, differential privacy noise, and US-skewed coverage) and of applying NewsGuard ratings longitudinally.
- Empirical evidence of stability in NewsGuard ratings over time (only 1–3% of domains change category), supporting retrospective classification.
- Documentation of age-based asymmetries in misinformation engagement in European settings, echoing US findings.
- Framing of the results as directly relevant to Digital Services Act transparency and data-access obligations.

## Methods

The authors replicate and adapt Aslett's (2022) public code implementing Guess et al. (2021), applying it to Meta URL Shares Dataset v10.1 filtered by `top_country` for DE, FR, IT, and the US. Domains were classified using an October 2022 NewsGuard snapshot (8,506 domains; score ≥60 = trustworthy). Representativeness of NewsGuard was assessed against the Lasser-Rupp list, Media Bias/Fact Check, and Grinberg et al. (2019). Confidence intervals account for differential privacy noise (σ=14 shares; σ=2,228 views), and Views/Shares are disaggregated across six age cohorts.

## Findings

- URL volume declined steadily across all three countries from 2019 to 2022; France consistently leads in volume, Germany trails (consistent with lower news-on-Facebook adoption).
- Untrustworthy URL shares converged around 13–14% across countries in 2019 but diverged by 2022: France ~11%, Germany ~21%, Italy 22% — with peaks aligning to national election years.
- Absolute Views and Shares of untrustworthy content peaked in 2020 and then declined, tracking the overall URL decline.
- The *proportion* of Views from untrustworthy sources stayed roughly stable despite rising untrustworthy URL and Share shares, suggesting platform-level dampening of exposure.
- Sharing of untrustworthy content skews older than exposure: 65+ dominates in France (28%), 55–64 in Germany (36%), 45–54 in Italy (33%); users under 34 play a marginal role.
- US data show a stronger correlation between NewsGuard's untrustworthy-domain coverage and the dataset's untrustworthy URL share than European data — a key reason for restricting comparative claims to within-EU.
