# Community Notes undermoderate polarizing content by design creating risks in electoral processes

> Bouchaud, P., & Ramaciotti, P. (2026). Community Notes undermoderate polarizing content by design creating risks in electoral processes. *Science Advances*, *12*. https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aee6932
>
> [View paper](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aee6932)

## Summary

This paper offers a large-scale empirical audit of X's Community Notes, the platform's bridging-based crowd moderation system, arguing that its core design systematically fails to moderate the content most in need of correction. Because notes are only shown publicly when they attract cross-partisan agreement along an inferred latent ideological dimension, precisely the most polarizing posts — including electorally salient ones — evade moderation. Drawing on 1.9 million notes and 135 million ratings through March 2025 across 13 countries, the authors show that this is not an operational bug but a structural feature of bridging algorithms, with tangible implications for electoral integrity worldwide.

## Key Contributions

- First large-scale, cross-national empirical evaluation of Community Notes as a global moderation infrastructure.
- Reframes the shortcomings of bridging-based moderation as a design-level rather than operational problem.
- Connects a specific platform governance mechanism to concrete democratic risks, particularly around elections.
- Provides comparative evidence on how moderation performance varies with national polarization contexts.

## Methods

- Analysis of the full Community Notes corpus: 1.9M notes and 135M ratings up to March 2025.
- Integration with ideological scaling data across 13 countries to characterize the partisan positions of note authors and raters.
- Comparative assessment of which notes achieve "helpful" status across content types and polarization environments.

## Findings

- Notes targeting highly polarizing content are systematically less likely to reach the cross-partisan consensus threshold required to be publicly displayed.
- The bridging mechanism's effectiveness varies across countries as a function of underlying ideological polarization.
- Electoral content falls disproportionately into the undermoderated category, exposing electoral processes to unchecked misleading claims.
- The failure mode is structural: it follows directly from requiring bipartisan agreement in polarized environments.
