# Locate alternative media in the digital news ecosystem: A cross-country comparative study of alternative-mainstream audience overlap networks

> Zhu, Q., Liang, F., & Li, G. M. (2026). Locate alternative media in the digital news ecosystem: A cross-country comparative study of alternative-mainstream audience overlap networks. *Digital Journalism*, 1–22. https://doi.org/10.1080/21670811.2026.2683342
>
> [View paper](https://doi.org/10.1080/21670811.2026.2683342)

## Summary

This paper examines how alternative news outlets are structurally positioned relative to mainstream outlets in the digital news ecosystems of six Western European countries (Norway, Sweden, Austria, Germany, France, Spain). Using audience overlap networks built from the 2020 Reuters Digital News Report and refined via backbone extraction, the authors argue that "alternativeness" is relational and context-dependent rather than inherent to outlets. They show that audience overlap is generally low, but its patterning is shaped by media system type and, crucially, by the degree of institutionalized political access enjoyed by (especially far-right) populist actors. Where populists are politically marginal but professional journalism is strong (Sweden), alternative outlets form a tight isolated enclave; where populists are mainstreamed into government, the alternative–mainstream boundary blurs.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces audience overlap network analysis (with disparity-filter backbone extraction) as a novel method for empirically mapping alternative–mainstream relations.
- Provides systematic cross-country comparative evidence linking media system type and populist political access to audience structure.
- Refines the theoretical concept of "alternativeness" as relational, fluid, and contextual, distinguishing individual-level news repertoires from outlet-level structural overlap.
- Identifies a specific configuration—strong professional journalism plus political marginalization of populists—as conducive to insulated alternative enclaves.

## Methods

Cross-country comparative design across six European media systems (Northern, Central, Southern types per Brüggemann et al.). Secondary analysis of the 2020 Reuters Digital News Report survey (N = 12,161), combined with a content analysis of 46 emerging online outlets coded for alternative financing, organization, and ideology (39 classified as alternative; ICR α = 1 on a 12-outlet subsample). Weighted undirected audience overlap networks among 257 outlets were built and reduced using the disparity filter (p < .05) in the R `backbone` package. Analyses computed global metrics (density, transitivity, assortativity, components) and node centralities (degree, betweenness, eigenvector), with Welch's t-tests comparing alternative vs. mainstream outlets and high- vs. low-populist-access country groups.

## Findings

- Audience overlap is generally low: backbone networks are sparse (density 0.01–0.09) with many isolated alternative outlets.
- Northern Type systems (Norway, Sweden) show the densest networks and positive assortativity—alternative outlets cluster among themselves (H1-1 supported).
- Contrary to expectation (H1-2 rejected), alternative outlets in Northern systems occupy more central positions; in Sweden they even surpass mainstream outlets in average degree centrality, though mainstream retain higher eigenvector centrality.
- Greater institutionalized populist access (Austria, Norway, France) corresponds to more alternative–mainstream overlap, e.g., Resett.no/Document.no with Nettavisen; Unzensuriert.at/Kontrast.at with ORF.at (H2-1 supported).
- No statistically significant differences in audience size or centrality between high- and low-access country groups (H2-2 rejected).
- Sweden uniquely hosts a tightly connected, disconnected enclave of seven alternative outlets.
- Spain is highly fragmented (36 components); a notable bridge is the fact-checker Maldita.es sharing audiences with El País.
