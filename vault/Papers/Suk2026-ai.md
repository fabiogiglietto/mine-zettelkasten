# From mass media and social media to AI: A multilevel framework for understanding trust in generative AI

> Suk, J., & Zhang, Y. (2026). From mass media and social media to AI: A multilevel framework for understanding trust in generative AI. *Journal of Broadcasting & Electronic Media*, 1–24. https://doi.org/10.1080/08838151.2026.2694048
>
> [View paper](https://doi.org/10.1080/08838151.2026.2694048)

## Summary

This conceptual paper argues that public trust in generative AI should be understood through a multilevel framework informed by decades of communication research on trust in mass media and social media. Rather than treating generative AI as a wholly novel object of trust, the authors position it as the latest step in a longer trajectory of trust dynamics in information and communication technologies, and propose that scholars integrate insights across these prior domains to guide empirical work on AI. The contribution is theoretical: a synthesis and a research agenda rather than new data.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a multilevel conceptual framework for studying public trust in generative AI.
- Bridges established communication scholarship on mass media and social media trust with emerging questions about AI trust.
- Articulates a research agenda that treats generative AI as continuous with, but distinct from, prior media trust objects.
- Argues explicitly that trust in generative AI is a related-but-distinct phenomenon requiring its own analytic scaffolding.

## Methods

Conceptual and theoretical synthesis. The authors review prior literature on trust in mass media and social media, extract lessons from the trajectory of that research, and translate them into a multilevel framework applicable to generative AI. No empirical data are reported.

## Findings

- The paper is framework-building rather than empirical; specific analytical claims about the levels of trust are not detailed in the abstract.
- The central takeaway is programmatic: multilevel analysis is presented as the appropriate lens for future empirical inquiry into generative AI trust.
- Lessons from prior media trust research are argued to be portable — and instructive — for anticipating dynamics in AI trust.
