# Coordinated Inauthentic Behavior

## From Content to Coordination: A Methodological Turning Point

The papers gathered here trace an arc that begins with a deliberate reframing of disinformation research: away from content veracity and individual bad actors, toward the *collective behaviour* through which problematic information is amplified. [[Giglietto2020-9d8acdd7]] sets the foundational move with Coordinated Link Sharing Behavior (CLSB) during Italian elections, arguing for an ecological, action-based detection paradigm and operationalising Facebook's vague notion of "coordinated inauthentic behavior" as something empirically tractable. [[Giglietto2022-0e951ac5]] extends this into the pandemic infodemic, showing that CLSB-based mapping surfaces problematic content faster than fact-checkers can — and uncovering how Italian covid-skeptic networks grew as offshoots of pre-existing political clusters. [[Giglietto2023-fa71a001]] then closes the methodological loop with a continuously-updating workflow that recognises that any static list of coordinated accounts decays rapidly as actors adapt.

## Refining and Contesting the Concept

As the construct travels, it begins to fracture under conceptual scrutiny. [[Thiele2025-ol]] argues that "coordinated inauthentic behavior" conflates inauthenticity with manipulative intent and is too platform-bound, proposing "Coordinated Social Media Manipulation" with a rational-choice typology built around scale, elaborateness, and disguise. [[Hurcombe2025-cs]] reads the same vocabulary critically, showing how Meta's Newsroom mobilises terms like "authenticity" and "coordination" as floating signifiers that deflect responsibility from platform design onto outside bad actors. [[Graham2026-fb]]'s Reddit "botting" study problematises the human/bot binary itself, recasting authenticity as a culturally negotiated performance — a stance that resonates with [[Jovanovic-Harrington2026-ze]]'s account of how Serbia's SNS networks *mimic* grassroots support so effectively that standard detectors fail. Across these works, the founding concept is shown to be both indispensable and unstable.

## Methodological Pluralism in Detection

A substantial cluster pushes the technical frontier. [[Mannocci2025-ig]] systematically compares five multimodal operationalisations on UK 2019 election data, finding a fundamental trade-off between integration strength and inclusiveness, with multiplex community detection emerging as the most balanced option. [[Iannucci2025-eg]] complements this by adding a temporal decay kernel to a multiplex framework, benchmarking favourably against the field on 26 labelled datasets. [[Yang2025-iv]] sidesteps the manipulable timing signal altogether by exploiting statistical regularities in link-sharing speed and frequency, while [[Minici2024-tf]]'s IOHunter applies graph foundation models for cross-campaign generalisation. [[Gerard2025-br]] makes a more radical move with CANE/t-CANE, reconstructing user networks from latent narrative affiliations rather than interactions — a response to the API collapse that has eroded interaction-based methods. [[Pante2025-pq]] turns these refined methods back on the literature itself, reassessing claims of inter-state coordination once proper controls and multi-signal indicators are applied.

## Platform Expansion Beyond Twitter/X

The literature's historical concentration on Twitter is now being actively dismantled. [[Luceri2025-tr]] adapts CIB detection to TikTok's video-first ecosystem; [[Kansaon2025-id]] ports the logic into Brazilian WhatsApp groups during the 2022 election, linking coordinated messaging to offline mobilisation including the January 8 attacks; [[Kuznetsova2025-nu]] traces forwarding-based coordination among pro-regime Telegram channels in Russia and Belarus; [[Rodriguez_Farres2025-sg]] tackles real-time bot coordination on Bluesky. Each platform forces methodological adaptation — encrypted closed groups, message forwarding, decentralised protocols, video as primary modality — confirming that coordination tactics are platform-vernacular all the way down.

## Election Studies and Comparative Cases

Empirical longitudinal work continues to anchor the field in concrete electoral contexts. [[Oprea2025-lf]] documents hyperactive-user amplification across Romanian parties during the 2024 EP elections; [[Gaw2025-ru]] reconceptualises Philippine 2022 influence operations as political brokerage embedded in political-economic infrastructures; [[Poliakoff2026-fa]] uses OSINT on 350 ex-employee CVs to reconstruct the IRA as a labour-market actor in the Russian media economy. [[Graham2025-gp]]'s analysis of #IStandWithPutin argues that propaganda exploits rather than evades the platform infrastructures of truth — a thesis echoed in [[Giglietto2026-9b6a992d]]'s study of how coordinated gambling promotion exploits the regulatory grey zone between paid and organic content, intensified post-ChatGPT by generative AI aesthetics.

## Effects, Influence, and Sceptical Reassessment

Parallel to detection advances runs a growing scepticism about *impact*. [[Appel2026-qr]], working with FIES platform data, finds that 49 deceptive networks reached 37 million US Facebook users — but that exposure was extraordinarily concentrated, indirect via organic reshares, and largely uncorrelated with attitudinal outcomes once pre-exposure characteristics are controlled. [[Di-Marco2025-aa]] reaches a structurally compatible conclusion via graph theory: observed coordinated accounts in UK retweet cascades are placed essentially randomly and exert roughly a tenth of the influence an optimally-placed set could achieve. [[Arceneaux2026-xk]] offers a partial counterpoint, showing that bots act as agenda-builders particularly for *attribute* (negative-sentiment) salience among campaigns, though not the press. Zhao2025-iv — sorry, [[Zhao2025-ny]] — situates hashtag hijacking as a strategic interaction of attention, narrative, and bots. Together these works suggest CIB matters more for the structure of discourse than for direct persuasion.

## Coordination Beyond Malice

Several papers complicate the assumption that coordination equals manipulation. [[Song2025-yh]] shows that both pro- and anti-vaccine communities engage in CLSB during COVID-19, with credible public-health institutions just as willing to coordinate as misinformation networks — the behaviour is ideologically agnostic. [[Efstratiou2026-ij]] finds a predominantly contrarian coordinated retweet network amplifying credentialed anti-consensus experts in COVID science communication, while mainstream superspreaders operate without coordination. [[Kulichkina2026-zk]] documents how Chinese protest-era Twitter discourse hosted *both* mobilisation and state-aligned repressive coordination, underscoring social media's dual-use character in authoritarian contexts. Coordination, in these accounts, is a generic affordance whose normative valence depends on actors and goals.

## The AI Horizon

A final, forward-looking cluster confronts what generative AI does to this whole research programme. [[Orlando2025-ul]] simulates networked LLM agents and finds that mere teammate awareness — not explicit deliberation — is sufficient to produce coordination dynamics indistinguishable from explicit collective decision-making, including emergent strategies like cross-promotion and unified messaging. [[Schroeder2026-im]] generalises the threat to "malicious AI swarms" with persistent identities, adaptive coordination, and "LLM grooming" of future training data. [[Giglietto2026-9b6a992d]] provides empirical grounding: an exponential post-volume increase in coordinated gambling networks dated to the ChatGPT launch, with a structural break in mid-2023. [[FitzGerald2025-nv]] complements this by examining how manipulation campaigns persist through appropriation of emerging events — a dynamic generative tooling can only accelerate.

## Synthesis

Taken together, the corpus traces a maturing research line. The Italian CLSB work [[Giglietto2020-9d8acdd7]], [[Giglietto2022-0e951ac5]], [[Giglietto2023-fa71a001]] established the behavioural-detection paradigm; subsequent methodological pluralism ([[Mannocci2025-ig]], [[Iannucci2025-eg]], [[Yang2025-iv]], [[Gerard2025-br]], [[Minici2024-tf]]) refined the tooling; platform-comparative work pushed beyond Twitter; conceptual critiques ([[Thiele2025-ol]], [[Hurcombe2025-cs]], [[Graham2026-fb]]) destabilised the founding vocabulary; effects-oriented studies ([[Appel2026-qr]], [[Di-Marco2025-aa]]) called for humility about actual impact; and AI-era work ([[Orlando2025-ul]], [[Schroeder2026-im]], [[Giglietto2026-9b6a992d]]) reopened the threat model. The field's centre of gravity has moved from *detecting bad accounts* to *understanding coordination as a sociotechnical phenomenon* whose effects, motivations, and platform-vernacular forms must be theorised together.
