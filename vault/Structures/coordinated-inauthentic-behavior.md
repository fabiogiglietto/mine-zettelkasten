# Coordinated Inauthentic Behavior

## From Inauthentic Content to Coordinated Behaviour

The Zettelkasten under this topic traces an arc that begins with a methodological pivot: away from content-veracity and bad-actor detection toward the *behavioural* signature of coordination itself. [[Giglietto2020-9d8acdd7]] is the foundational move, operationalising "coordinated link sharing behaviour" (CLSB) on Facebook during the 2018–2019 Italian elections and showing that networks sharing the same URLs near-simultaneously are disproportionately implicated in problematic information. [[Giglietto2022-0e951ac5]] extends this into a fact-checking workflow during the COVID-19 infodemic, while [[Giglietto2023-fa71a001]] formalises a continuous monitoring loop that iteratively surfaces new coordinated accounts from a known seed list — addressing the chronic problem that static lists decay as actors adapt or get suspended. Together these papers establish the CooRnet lineage as a content-agnostic, behaviour-first research program.

## Refining the Methodological Core

A second cluster pushes the detection apparatus forward technically. [[Mannocci2025-ig]] confronts the fact that real coordination is multimodal — across retweets, mentions, hashtags, URLs — and shows that the dominant practice of "flattening" layers loses structurally important nodes; multiplex community detection emerges as the best compromise. [[Iannucci2025-eg]] adds the temporal dimension, combining exponential decay kernels with multiplex layers and benchmarking against 26 labelled datasets. [[Yang2025-iv]] attacks an evasion vulnerability in the lineage directly, arguing that timing-based signals are trivially manipulable and proposing speed/frequency regularities as more robust alternatives. [[Minici2024-tf]] reframes detection as a graph foundation model problem, while [[Gerard2025-br]] abandons interaction graphs altogether in favour of latent narrative clusters, demonstrating that user-user discourse networks can be reconstructed from shared participation in topics rather than platform-specific signals. [[Di-Marco2025-aa]] provides a sobering counterweight: a post-hoc influence analysis suggesting that observed coordinated accounts in real cascades are placed nearly randomly and exert far less influence than optimal benchmarks would allow.

## Conceptual Critique and Definitional Work

Running parallel to the methodological refinement is a strand interrogating the concept of "coordinated inauthentic behaviour" itself. [[Hurcombe2025-cs]] reads Meta's Newsroom as a discursive operation in which "CIB," "authenticity," and "coordination" function as floating signifiers that conveniently externalise responsibility while diffusing into academic and regulatory vocabularies. [[Thiele2025-ol]] argues that CIB conflates inauthenticity with manipulative intent and proposes "coordinated social media manipulation" anchored in a rational-choice typology of principals, agents, and disguise strategies. [[Graham2026-fb]] approaches the same issue ethnographically through Reddit's mushroom-emoji "botting," showing that authenticity is a culturally negotiated performance rather than a technical property — a direct challenge to binary human/bot framings. [[Pante2025-pq]] in turn applies methodological scrutiny to prior claims about inter-state collaboration, illustrating how the field self-corrects through stronger control comparisons.

## Platform and Geographic Expansion

The original CooRnet apparatus was Facebook-and-Twitter-centric; a cluster of recent work pushes it onto other platforms. [[Luceri2025-tr]] adapts coordination detection to TikTok's video-first ecosystem; [[Rodriguez_Farres2025-sg]] addresses Bluesky; [[Kuznetsova2025-nu]] applies forwarding-network analysis to pro-regime Telegram channels in Russia and Belarus; and [[Kansaon2025-id]] extends "rapid spread network" detection into encrypted WhatsApp groups during the 2022 Brazilian election, linking online coordination directly to the January 8 insurrection. Geographic and political diversification is equally pronounced: [[Jovanovic-Harrington2026-ze]] documents Serbia's SNS as a mimetic, decentralised form of digital authoritarianism that evades standard detection; [[Kulichkina2026-zk]] examines the dual-use logic of coordination during China's 2022 protests; [[Gaw2025-ru]] reconceptualises Philippine election operations as political brokerage; and [[Oprea2025-lf]] documents hyperactive-user amplification in Romanian EP elections. [[Poliakoff2026-fa]] supplies the organisational counterpart through an OSINT study of the IRA as a labour-market actor with a five-level hierarchy — a useful corrective to content-only views of "troll factories."

## What Coordination Does

A further cluster asks what coordinated behaviour actually accomplishes. [[Song2025-yh]] shows, importantly, that CLSB is ideologically agnostic: pro-vaccine institutional actors coordinate too, complicating any equation of coordination with malice. [[Efstratiou2026-ij]] traces how a predominantly contrarian coordinated retweet network selectively amplifies credentialed anti-consensus experts and precedes news coverage. [[Goel2025-iq]] reveals a phenomenon invisible to domain-list approaches: co-sharing of *factually true* mainstream articles repurposed for misleading narratives. [[Zhao2025-ny]] examines hashtag hijacking as strategic narrative substitution, and [[Graham2025-gp]] argues through the #IStandWithPutin case that contemporary propaganda parasitises the infrastructures of truth rather than evading them. [[FitzGerald2025-nv]] adds the temporal-strategic point that campaigns persist by appropriating emerging events. Most strikingly, [[Appel2026-qr]] combines platform exposure data with survey panels to show that Meta-identified deceptive networks reached tens of millions of US users — but that most reach came via reshares by unaffiliated accounts, and that associations with downstream attitudes largely vanish after controlling for pre-exposure user characteristics. This dovetails with the skeptical findings of [[Di-Marco2025-aa]]: coordination is prevalent and detectable, but its causal weight in shaping discourse may be more modest than alarm-driven framings suggest.

## The Generative-AI Horizon

The most recent papers register a phase shift. [[Giglietto2026-9b6a992d]] documents an exponential post-volume explosion in coordinated gambling networks following ChatGPT's release, arguing that generative AI intensifies rather than replaces established persuasion strategies. [[Orlando2025-ul]] uses generative agent-based modelling to show that mere teammate awareness among LLM agents produces coordination nearly equivalent to explicit collective deliberation — coordination as an emergent property of distributed mutual awareness. [[Schroeder2026-im]] synthesises these threats into a policy framework around "malicious AI swarms," arguing that LLM-plus-agentic-architecture is a qualitative leap that detection alone cannot meet. This horizon loops back to the conceptual debates: if authenticity is culturally performed ([[Graham2026-fb]]) and coordination can emerge without explicit instruction ([[Orlando2025-ul]]), the field's foundational categories — inauthentic, coordinated, automated — will need further reworking even as the CooRnet/CooRTweet detection lineage continues to mature.
