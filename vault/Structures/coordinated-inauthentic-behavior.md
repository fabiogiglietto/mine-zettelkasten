# Coordinated Inauthentic Behavior

## The coordination turn in disinformation studies

A central thread running through these papers is the deliberate pivot away from content- and actor-based detection toward behavior-centered, ecological accounts of manipulation. [[Giglietto2020-9d8acdd7]] is the foundational move: rather than asking whether a piece of content is true or whether an account is a bot, it asks whether networks of accounts act *together* in ways that betray collective strategy. [[Giglietto2023-fa71a001]] extends this into a continuously updated workflow for the 2022 Italian election, treating coordination as a moving target rather than a fixed list. [[Giglietto2022-0e951ac5]] applies the same CooRnet logic to COVID-19 fact-checking, showing that behavioral signals can surface problematic content faster and at higher precision than traditional fact-checker pipelines. Together these papers articulate the programmatic claim that coordination is a more durable and tractable analytical unit than veracity or identity.

## Methodological proliferation and its trade-offs

Around this core, a methodological literature has matured rapidly, and increasingly self-critically. [[Yang2025-iv]] argues that timing-based detection — the dominant CLSB signal — is too easy to evade, and proposes statistical regularities in sharing speed and frequency as more robust signatures. [[Iannucci2025-eg]] and [[Mannocci2025-ig]] both push detection into multiplex, time-aware network representations, showing that single-modality methods miss coordination that spans retweets, hashtags, mentions, and URLs, while naïve flattening discards structurally crucial nodes. [[Minici2024-tf]] takes a different route, treating IO detection as a graph foundation model problem with cross-campaign generalization. [[Gerard2025-br]] reframes the entire question by replacing interaction-based networks with discourse-based ones, identifying narrative "bridge users" between Truth Social and X. [[Luceri2025-tr]] extends detection into TikTok's video-first ecosystem, and [[Rodriguez_Farres2025-sg]] does the same for Bluesky in real time — both arguing that platform affordances reshape what coordination even looks like. Critically, [[Pante2025-pq]] turns this methodological apparatus on the field's own prior claims, suggesting that earlier evidence of *inter-state* collaboration may not survive more rigorous multi-signal benchmarking.

## What the concept conceals: critical reframings

Several papers interrogate the conceptual scaffolding itself. [[Thiele2025-ol]] argues that Meta's "coordinated inauthentic behavior" rubric conflates inauthenticity with manipulative intent and is poorly suited to platforms without real-name policies, proposing instead a rational-choice attribution model built around scale, elaborateness, and disguise. [[Hurcombe2025-cs]] reads "CIB" itself as a discursive achievement, showing how Meta's Newsroom uses authenticity, advertising, technology, and enforcement as floating signifiers that externalize blame and naturalize Meta's role as solver rather than cause. [[Graham2026-fb]] pushes hardest against the human/bot binary, demonstrating through the Reddit mushroom-emoji case that "botting" is often participatory culture, with authenticity culturally negotiated rather than technically given. [[Graham2025-gp]] inverts the usual framing too: in #IStandWithPutin, propaganda exploits — rather than evades — the platform infrastructures of truth. [[Di-Marco2025-aa]] adds a striking empirical deflation, showing that coordinated accounts on X occupy positions in retweet cascades statistically indistinguishable from random placement, calling into question how *pivotal* CIB actually is in diffusion.

## Mapping the ecosystem: actors, motives, and infrastructures

A parallel line moves from detection toward thick description of who is coordinating and why. [[Poliakoff2026-fa]] reconstructs the IRA's organizational chart from leaked CVs, recasting Prigozhin's troll factory as an outsourced media-labor enterprise integrated into the Russian PR industry. [[Gaw2025-ru]] reads Philippine influence operations as a contemporary form of political brokerage embedded in patronage economies. [[Jovanovic-Harrington2026-ze]] documents how Serbia's SNS mimics grassroots support in ways that evade conventional bot-farm detection, while [[Kuznetsova2025-nu]] maps Russian and Belarusian Telegram amplification and [[Kulichkina2026-zk]] examines the dual-use character of coordinated activity around the 2022 China COVID protests. [[Appel2026-qr]], drawing on the FIES collaboration, makes the consequential argument that financially motivated operations producing political content deserve the same scrutiny as politically motivated CIB — and shows that reach is overwhelmingly driven by *reshares from non-network accounts*, not direct posting by the operations themselves.

## Coordination beyond the malicious: domain, ideology, and platform

Several papers complicate the assumption that coordination is intrinsically pathological. [[Song2025-yh]] applies CLSB to UK and US vaccine discourse and finds that pro-vaccine institutional actors coordinate at scales comparable to anti-vaccine networks; coordination is ideologically agnostic infrastructure. [[Efstratiou2026-ij]] shows that a predominantly contrarian coordinated retweet network in COVID science discourse amplifies credentialed-but-marginal experts, with news coverage trailing rather than leading superspreader activity. [[Goel2025-iq]] further dissolves the source/content boundary by demonstrating that mainstream news articles get *co-shared* with fake news to support misleading narratives — a phenomenon roughly twice the size of fake-news circulation alone. [[Zhao2025-ny]] examines hashtag hijacking as a contest over attention and narrative; [[Oprea2025-lf]] documents hyperactive-user amplification across all major Romanian parties; [[Kansaon2025-id]] extends coordination detection into WhatsApp and traces it to the January 8 attacks in Brasília. [[FitzGerald2025-nv]] complements these by emphasizing temporal persistence — campaigns survive by appropriating emerging events.

## The next frontier: generative AI and agentic coordination

A final cluster turns to what coordination becomes when generative AI enters the loop. [[Giglietto2026-9b6a992d]] documents a structural break in coordinated gambling promotion on Facebook coinciding with ChatGPT's release, showing how AI intensifies rather than replaces existing persuasion drivers and exploits Meta's asymmetric governance of paid versus organic content. [[Orlando2025-ul]] uses generative agent-based modeling to show that LLM agents reproduce key IO coordination dynamics — narrative convergence, synchronized amplification, hashtag diffusion — with mere teammate awareness, no explicit deliberation required. [[Schroeder2026-im]] generalizes the warning: agentic multi-agent AI swarms with persistent identities, cross-platform reach, and human-level mimicry constitute a qualitative leap that may render synchrony-based detection — the very basis of the CooRnet tradition — increasingly fragile. The arc of the topic thus closes by pointing back to its origin: if coordination is the analytical primitive, then a world of cheap, adaptive, mutually aware artificial coordinators is precisely the world in which that primitive must be rethought.
