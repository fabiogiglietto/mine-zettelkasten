# Generative AI and Media

## From Object of Hype to Infrastructural Project

The papers in this cluster converge on a common starting move: refusing to treat generative AI as a self-evident technical artifact and instead asking *what kind of object* it is for media and political-communication research. [[Vertesi2026-lv]] gives this stance its sharpest form, arguing that AI is best read as a "Project" — a world-building assemblage of capital, infrastructure, and network power — and that most critiques function as "decoys" distracting from this political economy. [[Helmond2026-ll]] makes the parallel infrastructural move from inside platform studies, reading Facebook's retirement of the Like button and Google's space-based compute ambitions as signs that platformization is entering an AI-centered phase ("Big AI"). [[Dodds2026-df]] sets the journalistic register for this, recasting AI hype not as discourse but as a structuring infrastructure that redistributes legitimacy and resources within newsrooms. Together these papers establish the cluster's underlying premise: generative AI is studied here less as a tool than as a sociotechnical-political formation.

## Imaginaries, Framing, and the Discursive Construction of GenAI

A second strand traces how that formation is talked into being. [[Wang2025-zy]] maps utopian/dystopian imaginaries across UK, US, Chinese, and Indian newspapers, while [[Nguyen2026-vm]] complicates the dominant critique of AI anthropomorphism by showing that mentalistic-agentic framings in 15 outlets are present but neither dominant nor uniformly uncritical — variation tracks editorial style more than region. [[Stanusch2026-ec]] zooms into the Altman firing as a controversy moment, identifying Longtermism, Techno-Hagiography, and Regulatory Ambivalence as industry strategies of "premediation" and "preclusion" that occupy the future to absorb present critique. [[Weinbrand2026-sf]] performs the same operation for Google's AI Overviews, showing how Google, journalists, and SEO actors contest the meaning of generative search while the user's voice is largely absent. Across these works, framing analysis is no longer about discourse-as-reflection but discourse-as-infrastructure — actively shaping which futures, harms, and accountabilities become thinkable.

## The Post-Social Media Condition

[[Tornberg2026-lc]] and its companion [[Tornberg2025-ir]] articulate the broadest paradigmatic claim: the "social media" framework is unraveling under three pressures — algorithmic recommendation displacing the social graph, generative AI substituting for user-generated content, and user retreat into semi-private spaces. The result is three distinct post-social formations (algorithmic broadcasting, micro-communities, AI-mediated communication) each requiring new methods. This dovetails with [[Helmond2026-ll]]'s reading of the Like button's demise and with [[Dierickx2026-tw]]'s argument that GenAI produces a new epistemic category — "emergent facts" — for which traditional fact-checking frameworks are insufficient. The shared diagnosis is that the infrastructural substrate of public communication is being rebuilt around inference rather than expression.

## Persuasion, Manipulation, and the Empirical Backlash

A cluster of large-scale experiments interrogates the persuasive power of conversational AI, often pushing back against alarmist framings. [[Hackenburg2025-dj]] finds that post-training and prompting matter more than scale or personalization, with information density as the central mechanism — but also documents a persuasion-accuracy trade-off. [[Lin2025-xp]] extends this across four elections, showing that LLM dialogues outperform traditional ads primarily through facts and evidence rather than psychological manipulation, while documenting a systematic accuracy gap for right-leaning advocacy. [[DiGiuseppe2026-pu]] adds a crucial political contingency: brief out-party bias warnings reduce LLM persuasion by ~25%, suggesting perceived neutrality is itself a lever. [[Hameleers2026-mc]] delivers a parallel deflation for visual disinformation, finding that AI-generated images do not universally outperform text or decontextualized video. The collective contribution is a sober empirical correction to Cambridge-Analytica-style speculation — though one that simultaneously establishes that real, measurable persuasion effects do exist.

## Synthetic Realities, Coordinated Harm, and Epistemic Security

Where the persuasion papers temper alarm, another strand documents emerging *systemic* harms. [[Emilio2026-ik]] proposes a four-layer "synthetic reality" stack and articulates the Generative AI Paradox: as synthetic media saturates the environment, rational discounting of all digital evidence raises the cost of truth itself. [[Schroeder2026-im]] identifies malicious AI swarms — LLMs plus agentic architectures — as a qualitative leap beyond prior botnets, while [[Orlando2025-ul]] provides the first generative agent-based simulation showing that mere teammate awareness suffices for coordinated influence behavior to emerge. [[Giglietto2026-9b6a992d]] grounds this empirically in coordinated Facebook gambling networks, documenting an exponential post-ChatGPT volume increase and AI-aestheticized persuasion that exploits Meta's organic/paid regulatory gap. [[Schiffrin_undated-gi]] translates these dynamics into policy, mapping deepfake financial fraud and arguing for gatekeeper liability over victim-focused regulation. [[Waight2026-ts]] adds a geopolitical layer: state-coordinated media leaks into LLM training data, producing measurably more pro-regime outputs in lower-media-freedom languages — institutional power inscribed in model weights.

## Generative AI as Method

The cluster also reflexively treats GenAI as a *methodological instrument*. [[Achmann-Denkler2026-lx]] shows that GPT-4o outperforms specialized computer-vision pipelines for face recognition and person-counting in Instagram political imagery, lowering the barrier to large-scale visual analysis. [[Arminio2025-tw]] makes a more conceptually ambitious case: VLLMs enable *connotative* (not just denotative) image clustering and produce interpretable cluster summaries, addressing the long-standing opacity of CNN-based pipelines. [[Triedman2025-uy]]'s analysis of Grokipedia exemplifies a third methodological mode — auditing AI-generated knowledge artifacts at scale, showing Grokipedia is heavily derivative of Wikipedia but cites blacklisted sources (Stormfront, InfoWars) at dramatically higher rates, especially on controversial topics. These papers collectively demonstrate that GenAI is simultaneously the field's object and instrument, raising methodological-reflexivity stakes the cluster has only begun to articulate.

## Tensions and Open Lines

Two productive tensions run across the corpus. First, between the *deflationary* empirical work on persuasion ([[Hackenburg2025-dj]], [[Lin2025-xp]], [[Hameleers2026-mc]]) and the *inflationary* systemic-risk arguments ([[Emilio2026-ik]], [[Schroeder2026-im]], [[Waight2026-ts]]): individual persuasive effects may be modest while systemic epistemic erosion is severe. Second, between studies that take GenAI's affordances at face value and the political-economic reframings of [[Vertesi2026-lv]], [[Dodds2026-df]], and [[Helmond2026-ll]], which would read even the methodological adoption of GPT-4o as enrollment into the Project of AI. The unresolved question — how to study generative AI *with* generative AI without legitimating it — is likely to organize the next phase of this research agenda.
