# Computational Methods and LLMs for Communication Research

## From Tools to Pipelines: The Computational Turn in Communication Research

The papers gathered here trace an unmistakable shift in computational communication research: away from single-model, single-modality classifiers and toward multi-stage *pipelines* in which LLMs, embeddings, and network methods are composed, validated, and critiqued as a connected workflow. What unites the collection is less a shared object of study than a shared methodological predicament — how to make machine-generated measurements credible enough to bear substantive claims about political discourse.

## LLMs as Measurement Instruments

A first cluster of work treats LLMs as general-purpose measurement devices for text. [[Le-Mens2025-qz]] shows that simply *asking and averaging* GPT-4 or Llama responses can scale political texts on policy dimensions across languages; [[DiGiuseppe2025-es]] extends this logic to open-ended survey responses via paired comparisons; [[Lee2026-je]] pushes further, demonstrating that LLMs can infer users' partisan alignment even from ostensibly nonpolitical chatter. [[Lai2024-to]] reminds us that latent-ideology estimation need not depend on LLMs at all — Reddit sharing patterns recover video-level slant — but the trend toward LLM-based scaling is clear. Domain adaptation extends the toolkit: [[Meher2025-qb]] shows that QLoRA fine-tuning of Llama-3 on consumer hardware outperforms both base LLMs and BERT-era baselines for conflict event coding, while [[Tan2024-vl]] surveys the broader landscape of LLM-based annotation and synthesis.

Yet the same papers that demonstrate capability also surface limits. [[Paci2025-ag]] finds that even GPT-4o-mini fails badly on implicit Italian political discourse, falling 20 points below human ceilings. [[DeVerna2025-dl]] shows that fact-checking accuracy collapses without curated retrieval — reasoning and built-in web search are not substitutes for evidence. [[Brown2025-jk]] complicates the bias discourse by showing that LLM annotation biases are dataset-specific rather than model-specific, and that item difficulty dwarfs demographic effects on agreement. Together these papers argue that LLMs are competent but conditional instruments: their reliability depends on task, context, and scaffolding.

## Pipelines and Their Validation

A second strand foregrounds the *pipeline* as the unit of methodological reflection. [[Marino2024-2fbc690f]] articulates this most explicitly, describing an LLMs-in-the-loop workflow (classification → embedding → clustering → labeling) for Italian political Facebook content and arguing that each stage demands its own validation protocol with expert — not crowdsourced — coders. [[Giglietto2024-cbeb3f70]] provides one such stage-level comparison, finding that OpenAI's text-embedding-3-large outperforms UmBERTo for clustering Italian news. [[Waight2025-al]] formalizes the estimand/estimator distinction for narrative similarity, showing that fine-tuned LLM pipelines recover Russian disinformation narratives that exact text-reuse methods miss entirely. [[Arminio2025-tw]] uses VLLMs as intermediate text generators to make image clustering both more *connotative* and more interpretable, while [[Achmann-Denkler2026-lx]] finds GPT-4o outperforming specialized computer-vision models for face recognition in campaign imagery. [[Arora2025-tx]] pushes framing analysis into multimodal terrain. The common move is to treat the LLM not as the answer but as one component whose outputs must be checked, often against another model or human gold standard.

[[Bailard2024-pj]] and [[Larsson2026-ro]] exemplify the pay-off when such pipelines are carefully validated: the former uses a fine-tuned DeBERTa to track collective action frames across 500,000 Proud Boys messages, the latter uses GPT-4 zero-shot classification on a decade of Norwegian Facebook posts. [[Iris2026-pg]] combines ChatGPT-4o entity extraction with fuzzy matching and manual checks to document disproportionate radical-right visibility across five EU countries. These are studies in which LLMs enable inferences that would have been impossible at scale a few years ago — but only because the authors invest heavily in stage-by-stage validation.

## The Critical Counter-Current

Running alongside these enthusiastic applications is a sharpening critique. [[Balluff2026-if]] mounts the most sustained case: LLM adoption in communication research has been "rapid but largely unreflective," and the field underestimates reproducibility risks from corporate model deprecation, language bias, environmental cost, and the fragility of prompts. [[Fan2025-ut]] addresses one technical face of this concern by showing that linear concept erasure (LEACE) can purge source- and language-confounds from embeddings — reframing a long-standing worry about "corpus-driven structure" as a problem of observed confounding. [[Ulloa2024-jm]] mounts a parallel critique of digital trace data itself: ex-situ web scraping introduces ~34% content disparity *regardless of time delay*, with errors systematically biased across news categories. The lesson across these papers is that methodological pluralism and reflexivity matter more than chasing the newest model.

## Networks, Coordination, and Cross-Platform Discourse

A third cluster pursues network-analytic questions about coordinated behavior and cross-platform flow, often in dialogue with the LLM-based methods above. [[Iannucci2025-eg]] and [[Mannocci2025-ig]] both argue that coordination is intrinsically *multiplex* and *temporal*, and that prevailing flatten-and-cluster approaches miss this structure; they converge on multiplex community detection as the most balanced operationalization. [[Yang2025-iv]] proposes statistical regularities in link-sharing speed and frequency as evasion-resistant alternatives to timing-only detectors. [[Minici2024-tf]] introduces graph foundation models for information operations, while [[Pante2025-pq]] uses these newer detectors to *reassess* prior claims of inter-state coordination, finding the evidence weaker than reported. [[Kulichkina2026-zk]] applies coordination detection to dual-use dynamics around China's COVID protests. Outside the coordination literature proper, [[Gerard2025-br]] reframes cross-platform influence as a *discursive* rather than interactional phenomenon via narrative-cluster embeddings, identifying a tiny set of "bridge users" who seed most Truth Social → X migrations. [[Bruns2025-fz]] generalizes the underlying intuition with "practice mapping" — using embeddings of network actions to escape the "hairball" problem of conventional visualization.

## New Estimands for Discourse

Finally, several papers extend the conceptual vocabulary of communication research by operationalizing previously elusive constructs. [[Elfes2026-jb]] introduces *narrative polarisation* via Greimas' actantial model executed by an LLM, finding that YouTube videos polarise much more than their comment sections. [[Sarmiento2025-as]] proposes unsupervised pipelines for emergent framing. [[Nenno2025-xa]] operationalizes news values cross-nationally to compare flagged misinformation across 24 countries. Gaisbauer2025-br argues for a multi-level "political cartography" of news sharing across story, outlet, and content. [[Allen2025-ot]], commenting on platform-independent experimentation via LLM-based feed reranking, situates these methodological innovations in the political economy of closing API access — a reminder that the pipelines surveyed here are not only technical artifacts but also responses to a constrained data environment.

## Where the Arc Points

Read together, these papers describe a field that has moved past the question of whether LLMs and embeddings *can* be used in communication research and into the harder questions of *how* and *with what safeguards*. The emerging consensus has three elements: pipelines, not single models, are the proper unit of analysis and validation; computational power must be paired with theoretical estimands (narrative similarity, narrative polarization, collective action frames, connotative meaning) rather than off-the-shelf benchmarks; and methodological choice should be evaluated against open-science, reproducibility, and infrastructural concerns, not only against accuracy scores. The most generative tensions ahead lie between the scaling enthusiasm of [[Le-Mens2025-qz]], [[Gerard2025-br]], and [[Meher2025-qb]] and the critical reflexivity demanded by [[Balluff2026-if]], [[Ulloa2024-jm]], and [[Paci2025-ag]] — a productive disagreement about what computational communication research should look like in the LLM era.
